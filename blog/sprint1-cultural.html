Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

When I was a SAP ABAP Developer, we have this process starting from user's request contacting functional team, functional team makes documentation and requirements then approved, documentation passed to development team to assess and create a program as requested. After program creation, developer then to alpha test, if all passed according to requirements, will be transported to QAS where functional team to beta test as user. If all goes well, document will be signed off then will be transported to production.

But not all projects went successfully to that process as sometimes, user's wants to add more features in the middle of project phase. This creates additional communications time-frames, document to sign-off again and heaps of testing which delays the project timeline.

Because this happens frequently specially on near go-live schedule. We as a team would sit-down with a user representative and would work as developer-user instead of developer-functional-user. Developer-functional-user sometimes there's a problem in between with communication. And usually the resistance is between developer and functional. There's one instance that the requirements is not documented properly as per user requests.

At the end, the transition between developer-user is much faster, we understand clearly on what user wants directly and testing results came back from user's in an instant. Once the project is done, and user thinks its ok, we then update the documentation and notify the functional team and approval team for the update. We received lesser complaints with this process.
